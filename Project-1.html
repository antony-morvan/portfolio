Overview

This page presents several 3D design and modeling projects I’ve carried out across my academic and professional experiences - ranging from missile design studies to composite blade optimization at Airbus.

Missile 3D Design

As part of my aerospace design coursework, I developed a full 3D model of a tactical missile using 3DExperience. In the frame of a full teoretical definition, the objective was to define an optimized aerodynamic shape, internal component arrangement, and surface continuity for aerodynamic efficiency. This included modeling the propulsion chamber, nozzle contour, and fin assemblies, ensuring realistic mass distribution and manufacturability.

Ariane 5 Structural Design Study

I worked on a 3D modeling study of the Ariane 5 launcher, focusing on the first stage structure and interstage configuration. Using 3DExperience, I analyzed the integration of tanks, boosters, and fairing connections while maintaining lightweight efficiency and structural rigidity. This project provided deep insight into complex launcher assemblies and structural design constraints at system level.

Cryogenic Test Bench Skid

During my internship at Ateliers Bigata, I took part in the complete 3D conception & optimization of a cryogenic test bench used for high-pressure liquid nitrogen and hydrogen testing. The model integrated over 300 unique components, including valves, insulation supports, pressure manifolds, and control & safety systems. This large-scale assembly required precise mechanical design coordination, tolerance management, and fluid integration consistency. I was responsible for both the system architecture and the CAD assembly definition used for manufacturing.

Helicopter blade component design

At Airbus, I contributed to the design of a new generation composite blade by applying Shape Generative Design methods within CATIA 3DExperience. This approach allowed the creation of optimized geometries for improved aerodynamic and structural performance. I performed surface continuity checks and mechanical integration verifications between generative components and conventional structures.

Historical Aircraft Restoration

As part of a project of heritage preservation initiative, I recreated in 3D a flight control component from the Deperdussin monoplane (1912), one of the earliest aircraft equipped with a closed cockpit and advanced control ergonomics. The objective was to reconstruct the original command linkage based solely on archival photographs and museum references, and to produce detailed manufacturing drawings for possible restoration or educational purposes.

Using CATIA V5, I developed a precise 3D model of the mechanical system including levers, joints, and pivot assemblies. The reconstruction required geometric interpretation, proportional scaling from historical imagery, and CAD constraint definition to replicate the original movement of the control column.
