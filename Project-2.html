Finite Element Analysis (FEA)

Structural Optimization and Stress Analysis across Aerospace Projects

Overview

Finite Element Analysis (FEA) has been a cornerstone of my engineering work, enabling the validation and optimization of complex aerospace structures under realistic load cases. From composite overbraiding systems to metallic assemblies, my FEA experience includes structural design validation, optimization, and weight reduction studies using CATIA 3DExperience and Abaqus.

Optimization of Flap Support – Airbus A350

Objective: Structural Analysis and Weight Reduction using Finite Element Method.

This study focused on the flap support system for the A350, seeking an optimal balance between weight and strength. Using 3DExperience, several design iterations were analyzed to ensure safety under certified load conditions.

A350 flap support FEM model

Process & Results

  • Performed detailed FEA simulations with a safety factor of 1.5 on yield strength.
  • Three optimized versions were compared for weight and stress performance:
    • Version 1: 63.7 kg – -3.4% Margin on SF.
    • Version 2: 64.98 kg – Best safety margin.
    • Version 3: 76.8 kg – strongest but less weight efficient.
  • The final design reduced mass while maintaining full compliance with the design criteria.

Tools & Key Takeaways

Software: CATIA 3DExperience (FEM module).
Skills: Lightweight structure optimization, stress analysis, design iteration workflow.
