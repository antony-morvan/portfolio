Space Systems Engineering & Mission Design

Simulation and analysis of interplanetary trajectories, Earth observation orbits, and satellite communications using AGI STK and analytical models.

Overview

This project focuses on the design and simulation of space missions using AGI Systems Tool Kit (STK), supported by analytical calculations in orbital mechanics and launcher performance evaluation. Several missions were modelled : from Earth-to-Moon transfer, Mars rendez-vous, to Earth observation and telecommunication constellations.

Mission Design with STK

Using STK 12, I developed complete space-mission profiles:

  • Design of a Lunar Transfer Mission with optimal injection and capture trajectories.
  • Mars Transfer planning using patched-conic approximation and ΔV budgeting.
  • Earth Observation Satellite setup with sun-synchronous orbit definition and revisit analysis.
  • Communication Constellation analysis including coverage and link-budget estimation.

Geosynchronous Mission simulations performed in AGI STK.

Analytical & Manual Calculations

In addition to software simulation, all orbit determination and launcher performance analyses were performed manually to validate the numerical results. Using classical astrodynamics equations and performance estimation methods, I calculated transfer ΔV, Tsiolkovsky trajectories, and vehicle mass ratios for preliminary launcher and satellite design studies
