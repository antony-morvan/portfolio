Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) Analysis

Aerodynamic and internal flow studies using ANSYS Fluent

Overview

This project gathers a set of CFD analyses carried out in ANSYS Fluent, covering both internal and external flow configurations. The main objectives were to evaluate aerodynamic efficiency, turbulence effects, and flow behavior through numerical simulation and physical interpretation.

Internal Flow Study — Dual-Stream Mixer Analysis

The dual-stream mixer was analyzed to study internal flow mixing performance and pressure losses within a complex geometry. The objective was to optimize mixing efficiency between primary and secondary streams while maintaining acceptable total pressure recovery.

Using ANSYS Fluent, I performed steady-state RANS simulations with a k–ε turbulence model, evaluating velocity gradients, swirl formation, and outlet uniformity. Streamline visualization and pressure maps were used to identify local determine mixing efficiency and evaluated loss coefficients to optimize design for minimal energy loss.

CFD Dual-Stream mixer model used in ANSYS Fluent.

Aerodynamic Analysis of a NACA 0012 Airfoil

This study involved a comprehensive CFD analysis of a NACA 0012 airfoil, focusing on both inviscid (ideal) and viscous flow conditions. Simulations were carried out using ANSYS Fluent to investigate aerodynamic characteristics such as pressure distribution, lift, drag, and flow separation under varying angles of attack.

CFD Post Processing on a NACA airfoil

Process & Achievements

  • Generated and refined computational meshes ranging from 15,000 to 40,000 elements for improved precision.
  • Simulated inviscid flow to analyze lift and pressure distribution across the profile.
  • Applied Spalart–Allmaras and k–ω SST models to capture viscous effects and boundary layer behavior.
  • Improved convergence and stability through mesh refinement, achieving lift and drag coefficients aligned with experimental benchmarks.
  • Analyzed lift coefficient evolution with varying angles of attack, identifying flow attachment and separation transitions.

CFD Analysis of Ariane 5 Launcher

As part of an aerodynamic and launch vehicle study, I conducted a CFD analysis on the Ariane 5 launcher to evaluate the external flow behavior during ascent and assess shock formation and pressure evolution along the vehicle body.

The objective was to identify critical aerodynamic zones, such as shock intersections and flow separation near the fairing and booster junctions. Using ANSYS Fluent and numerical post-processing tools, I visualized Mach contours, stagnation pressure fields, and temperature gradients to characterize the impact of compressibility at high-speed regimes.

Flow visualization and shock-wave structures around the Ariane 5 launcher.

Process & Insights

  • Generated a high-fidelity surface and volume mesh around the Ariane 5 geometry.
  • Simulated supersonic flow regimes to visualize detached and oblique shocks.
  • Analyzed pressure coefficient distributions to identify aerodynamic loads on key structures.
  • Correlated the results with analytical shock relations and flow models from compressible aerodynamics.

This study provided valuable insight into launcher aerodynamics under hypersonic flow, enabling comparison between numerical and theoretical results to validate modeling accuracy.
